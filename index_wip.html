About Me

Developing Vision-By-Energetics (ViBE) to realize supersensing.

I'm a Project Scientist in the Robotics Institute at Carnegie Mellon University, where I work on Thermal Computational Imaging with Prof. Srinivasa Narasimhan and Prof. Aswin Sankaranarayanan. My current research exposes heat as a carrier of visual information and develops novel vision and imaging systems that leverage the ubiquitous interplay of light and heat in the environment.

Previously, I obtained my Ph.D. and M.S. in Robotics from the University of Michigan, where I was advised by Prof. Matthew Johnson-Roberson and Prof. Ram Vasudevan. My doctoral research focused on thermal imaging for autonomous vehicles in adverse weather conditions, where I specifically focused on the unique physics of thermal cameras.

Prior to my Ph.D., I earned my B.Tech and M.Tech in Electrical Engineering from the Indian Institute of Technology, Madras.
